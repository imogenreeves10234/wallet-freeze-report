The freeze is a switch inside Tether's own software. Only Tether can flip it. No wallet app, no exchange, no "recovery expert", and no police officer can flip it directly. An ordinary fraud / police investigation

A sanctions case (e.g. North-Korea / "Lazarus" / Bybit-hack money)

Golden rule: act while the money is still frozen. Once a court starts deciding who gets it, your chance to speak up shrinks. Speed = leverage.

The second trap: the recovery-scam wave

People who've had funds frozen are hunted by a second wave of scammers who promise to "get it back". This is a bigger threat to you right now than the freeze itself, because it can cost you more money. Read this carefully.

The hard rules — memorise them
  • Nobody can unfreeze your wallet for a fee. It is technically impossible for anyone but Tether.
  • Tether will never contact you on Telegram, WhatsApp, or by DM. Real contact is only via @tether.to email or through law enforcement.
  • No real agency (FBI, police, court, "compliance department") ever charges you a fee to return your money.
  • Anyone who already "knows your exact frozen amount" and messages you first is a scammer working from a victim list — not a rescuer.
Walk away instantly if you see any one of these
  • They contacted you first (Telegram/WhatsApp/DM/comment).
  • Any upfront fee — "verification", "tax", "release", "security", "bank" — especially paid in crypto or gift cards.
  • A guarantee of success, or "your funds are already located and ready to release".
  • They claim to be from the FBI, FCA, a court, or "Tether compliance".
  • They ask you to connect your wallet to a site, "validate/sync" it, or share your secret phrase.
  • They refuse a video call or can't show a real, checkable licence.

The tokens sitting in your wallet with names like "UNFREEZE TG:…" are this scam in its purest form. They are bait. Do not visit any link in them, do not reply, do not pay. Just leave them there — they are harmless as long as you never interact with them.

The "never do this" list

  • Never pay any fee to "unfreeze", "verify", "release", or "tax" the funds.
  • Never send crypto or gift cards to any "agent", "officer", or "recovery service".
